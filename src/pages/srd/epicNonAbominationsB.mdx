<h5><a
href="http://www.wizards.com/dnd/images/EPIC_Gallery/Gallery5a/44170_C5_mithralgolem.jpg"><img
class="art-wide" src="pict.jpg" title="" alt="pict"
style="border: 0px solid ; width: 73px; height: 75px;" /></a>MITHRAL
GOLEM </h5>
<p>A mithral golem cannot speak or make any vocal noise, nor does
it have any distinguishable odor. Unlike most golems, it can run just
as well as a normal creature. </p>
<p>A mithral golem&#8217;s natural weapons are treated as epic for the
purpose of overcoming <a style="color: rgb(87, 158, 182);"
href="monsterTypes.html#DR">damage reduction</a>.</p>
<h6>COMBAT</h6>
<p><span style="font-weight: bold;">Alacrity (Su):</span> Once
per round, the mithral golem may take an extra standard action (either
before or after its other actions in the round). </p>
<p><span style="font-weight: bold;">Magic Immunity (Ex):</span> A
mithral golem is immune to all magical and supernatural effects, except
as follows. A <a style="color: rgb(87, 158, 182);"
href="spellsS.html#slow">slow</a> spell negates its alacrity for 1d4
rounds, while a <a style="color: rgb(87, 158, 182);"
href="spellsHtoL.html#haste">haste</a>
spell restores 1d6 hit points per level of the caster (maximum
10d6) or restores its alacrity, if previously negated by a slow spell.
Multiple slow spells simply extend the duration of the effect. </p>
<h6>Construction </h6>
<p>A mithral golem&#8217;s body is sculpted from 8 1/2 tons of pure
iron and is then polymorphed into mithral (using <a
style="color: rgb(87, 158, 182);"
href="spellsPtoR.html#polymorph-any-object">polymorph any object</a>).
A mithral golem costs 250,000 gold pieces to create, which includes
5,000 gp for the body. Assembling the body requires a successful&nbsp;<a
style="color: rgb(87, 158, 182);" href="skillsAll.html#craft">Craft</a>
(armorsmithing) or <a style="color: rgb(87, 158, 182);"
href="skillsAll.html#craft">Craft</a> (weaponsmithing) check (DC 30).
The creator
must be 25th level, have the <a style="color: rgb(87, 158, 182);"
href="epicFeats.html#craft-epic-magic-arms-and-armor">Craft Epic Magic
Arms and Armor</a> feat, and
be able to cast arcane spells. Completing the ritual drains 5,000 XP
from the creator and requires <a style="color: rgb(87, 158, 182);"
href="spellsFtoG.html#geas-quest">geas/quest</a>, <a
style="color: rgb(87, 158, 182);" href="spellsHtoL.html#haste">haste</a>,
and <a style="color: rgb(87, 158, 182);" href="spellsTtoZ.html#wish">wish</a>.
</p>
<p>


</p>
<h5>ADAMANTINE
GOLEM </h5>
<p>An adamantine golem cannot speak or make any vocal noise, nor
does it have any distinguishable odor. </p>
<p>An adamantine golem&#8217;s natural weapons are treated as epic for
the purpose of overcoming <a style="color: rgb(87, 158, 182);"
href="monsterTypes.html#DR">damage reduction</a>.</p>
<h6>COMBAT </h6>
<p><span style="font-weight: bold;">Trample (Ex):</span> As a
standard action during its turn each round, the adamantine golem can
literally run over an opponent at least one size category smaller than
itself. The trample deals 8d10+30 points of bludgeoning Damage.
Trampled opponents can either attempt attacks of opportunity at a -4
penalty or Reflex saves (DC 57) for half Damage. The DC is
Strength-based.</p>
<p><span style="font-weight: bold;">Magic Immunity (Ex):</span>
An adamantine golem is immune to all magical and supernatural effects.</p>
<h6>Construction </h6>
<p>An adamantine golem&#8217;s body is sculpted from 45,000 pounds of
pure iron and is then polymorphed into adamantine (using <a
style="color: rgb(87, 158, 182);"
href="spellsPtoR.html#polymorph-any-object">polymorph any
object</a>). The golem costs 500,000 gold pieces to create, which
includes
25,000 gp for the body. Assembling the body requires a successful <a
style="color: rgb(87, 158, 182);" href="skillsAll.html#craft">Craft</a>
(armorsmithing) or <a style="color: rgb(87, 158, 182);"
href="skillsAll.html#craft">Craft</a> (weaponsmithing) check (DC 40).
The creator
must be 30th level, have the <a style="color: rgb(87, 158, 182);"
href="epicFeats.html#craft-epic-magic-arms-and-armor">Craft Epic Magic
Arms and Armor</a> feat, and
be able to cast arcane spells. Completing the ritual drains 10,000 XP
from the creator and requires <a style="color: rgb(87, 158, 182);"
href="spellsC.html#crushing-hand">crushing hand</a>, <a
style="color: rgb(87, 158, 182);" href="spellsFtoG.html#geas-quest">geas/quest</a>,
and <a style="color: rgb(87, 158, 182);" href="spellsTtoZ.html#wish">wish</a>.


</p>

<p>A creature whose brain has been harvested by a brain collector
cannot be raised, reincarnated, or resurrected while the brain is in
the creature, because the collector preserves and draws upon the soul
and basic personality of the creature for as long as it retains the
brain. Neh-thalggus&#8217; own language is a silent sign language &#8220;spoken&#8221;
with their writhing head-tentacles. They can also communicate
telepathically with any creature that has a language within 100 feet. </p>
<p>A neh-thalggu&#8217;s natural weapons are treated as epic for the
purpose of overcoming <a style="color: rgb(87, 158, 182);"
href="monsterTypes.html#DR">damage reduction</a>.</p>
<h6>COMBAT </h6>
<p><span style="font-weight: bold;">Poison (Ex):</span> A
neh-thalggu&#8217;s bite injects a debilitating poison (DC 32) that Damages
the victim&#8217;s Constitution. He or she loses half his or her current
Constitution immediately on a failed save and must save again 1 minute
later or lose half of his or her remaining Constitution. If the
character survives, he or she heals the ability <a
style="color: rgb(87, 158, 182);"
href="abilitiesAndConditions.html#ability-damaged">Damage</a> at the
normal
rate (1 point per day). The save DC is Constitution-based.</p>
<p><span style="font-weight: bold;">Head-Tentacles (Ex):</span>
The long, whiplike tentacles that frame either side of a brain
collector&#8217;s face can deliver a dangerous touch attack that, if
successful, causes the target to dehydrate and wither (2d10 points of
desiccation Damage). In addition, each successful tentacle attack <a
style="color: rgb(87, 158, 182);"
href="abilitiesAndConditions.html#ability-drained">drains</a> 1 point
of Strength, 1 point of Dexterity, and 1 point of
Constitution. This is permanent ability drain and may only be reversed
by spells such as <a style="color: rgb(87, 158, 182);"
href="spellsPtoR.html#restoration">restoration</a> and <a
style="color: rgb(87, 158, 182);"
href="spellsPtoR.html#greater-restoration">greater restoration</a>. It
can bring all
ten tentacles to bear on a single target facing it or divide its
attacks against up to ten targets that it threatens. </p>
<p><span style="font-weight: bold;">Extract Brains (Sp):</span>
Once every 1d4 rounds, as a full-round action, a brain collector can
extract the brain from a target creature in line of sight. This attack
is psionic in nature and can be blocked by a <a
style="color: rgb(87, 158, 182);"
href="spellsDtoE.html#dimensional-anchor">dimensional anchor</a>
currently in force on the target. The target gets a Will save (DC 31)
to resist the extraction. The save DC is Intelligence-based. If the
save fails, his or her brain is drawn out intact through the skull by
extradimensional means and sucked up by the brain collector, lodging in
an unused storage sac above and behind its eyes. If the save succeeds,
he or she takes 9d6 points of Damage and is <a
style="color: rgb(87, 158, 182);"
href="abilitiesAndConditions.html#stunned">stunned</a> for 1d4+1
rounds. A
brain collector prefers to absorb brains of high-level arcane
spellcasters but is fully capable of extracting those of other foes as
a highly effective attack. When encountered, assume a neh-thalggu has a
full retinue of thirteen stolen brains. Each brain less than the full
thirteen bestows one <a style="color: rgb(87, 158, 182);"
href="abilitiesAndConditions.html#energy-drain">negative level</a> on
the neh-thalggu (though these
never convert to actual level loss), which is a powerful incentive for
the creature to always keep its brain-sacs filled. A neh-thalggu is
free to draw on all the&nbsp;<a style="color: rgb(87, 158, 182);"
href="skillsAll.html#knowledge">Knowledge</a> skills of each brain it
currently
stores, using the base ranks in a skill possessed by each brain, and
adjusted by the neh-thalggu&#8217;s own skill modifiers for the Knowledge
skill in question (or its Intelligence modifier, for a skill it has no
ranks in). </p>
<p><span style="font-weight: bold;">Dimensional Travel (Sp):</span>
A brain collector&#8217;s preferred method of locomotion is via <a
style="color: rgb(87, 158, 182);" href="spellsDtoE.html#dimension-door">dimension
door</a>, which it can do as a quickened action, once per round. It can
also use <a style="color: rgb(87, 158, 182);"

style="color: rgb(87, 158, 182);" href="spellsPtoR.html#plane-shift">plane

move-equivalent action.</p>
<p><span style="font-weight: bold;">Spells (Sp):</span> A fully

style="color: rgb(87, 158, 182);" href="sorcerer.html">sorcerer</a>

<p><span style="font-weight: bold;">Incorporeal:</span> A
neh-thalggu is not wholly in our reality but always remains partially

objects at will, and its own attacks pass through armor (except for its
bite attack, which is treated as if a corporeal attack). It always

<h5>CREATING A PARAGON CREATURE </h5>
<p>&#8220;Paragon&#8221; is a template that can be added to any creature
(referred to hereafter as the base creature). </p>
<p>The base creature&#8217;s type remains unchanged. The paragon
creature uses all the base creature&#8217;s statistics and special abilities
except as noted here. </p>
<p><span style="font-weight: bold;">HD:</span> A paragon creature
always has maximum hit points. Paragon creatures also gain an
additional 12 hit points per HD. </p>
<p><span style="font-weight: bold;">Speed:</span> A paragon
creature&#8217;s speed triples, for all movement types. </p>
<p><span style="font-weight: bold;">AC:</span> Paragon creatures
gain a +12 insight bonus to AC and a +12 luck bonus to AC. They also
gain a +5 natural armor bonus (if the creature already has natural
armor, use whichever is better). </p>
<p><span style="font-weight: bold;">Attacks:</span> A paragon
creature makes all its attacks with a +25 luck bonus on the attack
roll. </p>
<p><span style="font-weight: bold;">Damage:</span> A paragon
creature gains a +20 luck bonus on Damage rolls for all melee and
thrown ranged attacks. </p>
<p><span style="font-weight: bold;">Special Attacks:</span> A
paragon creature&#8217;s special attacks, if any, all gain a +13 insight
bonus, if applicable. The +13 insight bonus may only be applied to a
given special ability once. </p>
<p><span style="font-weight: bold;">Spell-Like Abilities (Sp) or
Psionics (Sp):</span> If the base creature has spell-like abilities, it
gains +15 to its caster level to use those abilities. A paragon
creature also gains the ability to use <a
style="color: rgb(87, 158, 182);"
href="spellsDtoE.html#greater-dispel-magic">greater dispel magic</a>, <a
style="color: rgb(87, 158, 182);" href="spellsHtoL.html#haste">haste</a>,
and
see <a style="color: rgb(87, 158, 182);"
href="spellsHtoL.html#invisibility">invisibility</a> three times per
day, even if it did not have
spell-like abilities before, at 15th caster level. </p>
<p><span style="font-weight: bold;">Special Qualities:</span> A
paragon creature retains all the special qualities of the base creature
and also gains the following.


</p>
<ul>
<li><a style="color: rgb(87, 158, 182);"
href="monsterTypes.html#resistance-to-energy">Fire and cold resistance</a>
10. If the creature already
possesses such resistance, use whichever is better.</li>
<li><a style="color: rgb(87, 158, 182);"
href="monsterTypes.html#DR">Damage reduction</a> 10/epic. If the
creature already possesses
Damage reduction, use whichever is better.</li>
<li><a style="color: rgb(87, 158, 182);"
href="monsterTypes.html#SR">Spell resistance</a> equal to the paragon
creature&#8217;s CR +25. If
the creature already possesses spell resistance, use whichever is
higher.</li>
<li><a style="color: rgb(87, 158, 182);"
href="monsterTypes.html#fast-healing">Fast healing</a> 20. If the
creature already possesses fast
healing, use whichever is better.</li>
<li>A paragon creature&#8217;s natural weapons are treated as epic
for the purpose of overcoming <a style="color: rgb(87, 158, 182);"
href="monsterTypes.html#DR">damage reduction</a>.</li>
</ul>
<p> </p>
<p><span style="font-weight: bold;">Saves:</span> The paragon
creature gains a +10 insight bonus on all its saving throws. </p>
<p><span style="font-weight: bold;">Abilities:</span> All ability
scores are 15 points higher than those of the base creature. </p>
<p><span style="font-weight: bold;">Skills:</span> The paragon
creature gains a +10 competence bonus on all its skill checks. </p>
<p><span style="font-weight: bold;">Feats:</span> Same as the
base creature, plus one bonus feat. </p>
<p><span style="font-weight: bold;">Climate/Terrain:</span> Any
land and underground. </p>
<p><span style="font-weight: bold;">Organization:</span> Same as
the base creature. </p>
<p><span style="font-weight: bold;">Challenge Rating:</span> As
base creature +15.</p>
<p><span style="font-weight: bold;">Treasure:</span> Standard for
a creature of the adjusted CR. </p>
<p><span style="font-weight: bold;">Alignment:</span> Same as the
base creature. </p>
<p><span style="font-weight: bold;">Advancement:</span> Same as
the base creature.

<h5>WORM THAT WALKS CHARACTERS </h5>
<p>By making eldritch preparations on a burial plot, a powerful
spellcaster can improve the likelihood of a worm that walks emerging
from the ground. The body is buried in an elaborate ritual that, the
spellcaster hopes, will attract a critical mass of worms or maggots.
The spellcaster performing the ritual must spend 10,000 gp in rare
reagents for the ceremony. Furthermore, the ritual drains 2,000 XP from
the spell-caster and requires the following spells: <a
style="color: rgb(87, 158, 182);" href="spellsHtoL.html#limited-wish">limited
wish</a>, <a style="color: rgb(87, 158, 182);"
href="spellsPtoR.html#polymorph-any-object">polymorph any object</a>, <a
style="color: rgb(87, 158, 182);" href="spellsS.html#summon-swarm">summon
swarm</a> (heightened to 7th level), and <a
style="color: rgb(87, 158, 182);" href="spellsS.html#sympathy">sympathy</a>.
Even if the ritual is performed correctly, there is only a
chance that the deceased spellcaster will arise as a worm that walks.
For each prepared but uncast arcane spell the deceased had at the
moment of death (or unused spell slots if a spontaneous caster),
there&#8217;s a 1% chance that a worm that walks will slither from the grave
soil in 1d4 days. Some worms that walk arise spontaneously from
ordinary burial plots, but such an event is exceedingly rare. And an
epic spell can create worms that walk every time without fail.
