<a id="power-stones"></a><h3>POWER STONES</h3>
<table border="0" cellpadding="0" cellspacing="0" style="width: 100%">
<tbody>
<tr>
<td></td>
</tr>
</tbody>
</table>
A power stone stores a power (or collection of powers). A stored power
can be used only once, &#8220;flushing&#8221; the stone. Using a power stone is
basically like manifesting a power.
<a id="power-stone-physical-description"></a><p><span style="font-weight: bold;">Physical Description:</span>
A power stone is a thumb-sized chunk of crystal that possesses a barely
detectable interior glow if it holds a low-level power. A stone
imprinted with more than one power or a higher-level power glows more
brightly, but never enough to provide illumination in its own right. A
typical power stone has AC 7, 5 hit points, a hardness of 8, and a
break DC of 20.</p>
<a id="power-stone-activation"></a><p><span style="font-weight: bold;">Activation:</span> To
activate a power stone, a manifester must mentally address it, as
described below. Once the power is known, the power stone is treated as
a power completion item, except as noted below.</p>
<a id="addressing-the-stone"></a><p><span style="font-weight: bold;">Addressing the Stone:</span>
A power stone must be &#8220;addressed&#8221; before a character can use it or know
exactly what power it contains. Doing this requires a successful <a
href="psionicSkills.html#psicraft" style="color: rgb(87, 158, 182);">Psicraft</a>
check (DC 15 + power
level). Once a particular power stone has
been addressed, it is not necessary to do so again. Addressing a power
stone in advance lets a character proceed directly to the next step
when the time comes to use it.</p>
<p>Using a stone&#8217;s stored power after addressing the stone
requires holding the stone and willing the power&#8217;s manifestation, as if
manifesting a power normally (a standard action). Activating a power
stone is subject to disruption just as manifesting a power normally
would be.</p>
<p>Additionally, the user must meet the following requirements.</p>
<ul>
<li>
<p>The user must have the power on his or her class list.</p>
</li>
<li>
<p>The user must have the requisite key ability score.</p>
</li>
</ul>
<p>If the user meets these requirements and has a manifester
level at least equal to the power&#8217;s manifester level, she can
automatically manifest the stored power without a check. If she meets
both requirements but her own level is lower than the power stone&#8217;s
manifester level, she has to make a manifester level check (1d20 +
user&#8217;s level), against a DC equal to the power stone&#8217;s manifester level
+1, to manifest the power successfully. On a failure, the user must
succeed on a DC 5 Wisdom check to avoid brainburn (see below). A
natural roll of 1 on this check is always a failure.</p>
<a id="determine-effect"></a><p><span style="font-style: italic;">Determine Effect:</span> A
power successfully manifested from a power stone works exactly as if
manifested normally. Assume the power stone&#8217;s manifester level is
always the minimum level required to manifest the power for the
character who imprinted the stone, unless the creator specifically
desires otherwise.</p>
<p>A power stone&#8217;s glow fades when its stored power is flushed.</p>
<a id="brainburn"></a><p><span style="font-style: italic;">Brainburn:</span> When
something goes awry, the user of a power stone improperly manifests the
stored power. The resultant psionic surge persists for 1d4 rounds,
unless the power stone is flung farther than 100 feet away or is
destroyed. This surge deals 1d6 points of damage per power stored per
round and arcs across multiple targets, similar to <a
href="psionicPowersDtoF.html#energy-current"
style="color: rgb(87, 158, 182);">energy current</a>,
except the damage type isn&#8217;t specified. The user is the primary target,
and one nearby ally is the secondary target (chosen at random).</p>
<p>The manifester level for a standard power stone is the minimum
manifester level needed to manifest the power (unless otherwise
specified).</p>

<a id="psicrowns"></a><h3>PSICROWNS</h3>
<table border="0" cellpadding="0" cellspacing="0" style="width: 100%">
<tbody>
<tr>
<td></td>
</tr>
</tbody>
</table>
A psicrown is a special headband that stores several powers. Unlike
dorjes, which can contain a wide variety of powers, each psicrown is of
a certain kind and holds specific powers. Unless otherwise stated, a
psicrown has power points equal to 50 x its manifester level when
created. These power points can be spent only on the powers that are
contained in the psicrown.
<a id="psicrown-physical-description"></a><p><span style="font-weight: bold;">Physical Description:</span>
A typical psicrown is large enough to fit on the head of a Medium
creature. Some psicrowns are actually crownlike and heavily adorned,
while others are simple headbands with crystal centerpieces. All
psicrowns weigh less than 1 pound. Most psicrowns are metallic, but one
could potentially be composed of any material. Metallic psicrowns have
AC 10, 10 hit points, hardness 8, and a break DC of 28. Psicrowns made
of psionically reinforced silk have AC 7, 10 hit points, hardness 2,
and a break DC of 24. Psicrowns can be made from other, more exotic
materials, but all have at least the strength and durability of
psicrowns made of psionically reinforced silk.</p>
<a id="psicrown-activation"></a><p><span style="font-weight: bold;">Activation:</span> Psicrowns
use the power trigger activation method, so manifesting a power from a
psicrown is usually a standard action that does not provoke attacks of
opportunity. (If the power being manifested, however, has a longer
manifesting time than 1 standard action, it takes that long to manifest
the power from a psicrown.) When activating a psicrown, the user spends
power points contained in the psicrown on any one of the psicrown&#8217;s
powers. Powers activated from a psicrown can be augmented with
additional power points from the psicrown itself, so long as the total
number of power points spent does not exceed the user&#8217;s manifester
level. The user can&#8217;t manifest a power from a psicrown using his own
power points.
</p>

<a id="psicrown-descriptions"></a><h6>Psicrown Descriptions</h6>

into one item and because they use the wearer&#8217;s ability score and

his manifester level when activating the power of a psicrown if it&#8217;s
higher than the manifester level of the psicrown. </p>
<p>The minimum manifester level of a psicrown is 8th. The
manifester level of a psicrown cannot be more than five levels higher
than the minimum manifester level for the highest-level power in the
