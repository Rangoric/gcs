<p><a href="http://www.wizards.com/dnd/images/UnA_Gallery/79155.jpg"><img
class="art-wide" src="pict.jpg" title="" alt="pict"
style="border: 0px solid ; width: 73px; height: 75px;" /></a>
A number of the standard character classes represent very specific (if not
always specialized) roles in the game. These classes &mdash; the <a style="color: #579eb6" href="#prestige-bard">bard</a>, the
<a style="color: #579eb6" href="#prestige-paladin">paladin</a>, and the <a style="color: #579eb6" href="#prestige-ranger">ranger</a> &mdash; might require special training to enter, training
that can be mimicked by transforming these classes into prestige classes.
</p>
<p>
With this variant, these classes become off limits for starting
(1st-level) characters. Thus, the three prestige classes presented below
represent elite character archetypes, roles that can only he achieved
through training in specific areas of knowledge and expertise. In each
case, the prestige class recreates the feel of the character class, but
with slight variations. Each class has fifteen levels, allowing a
character who enters at 6th level to continue along that path all the way
up to 20th level.
</p>
<p>
If you use any of the variant classes presented here, the standard version
of the class should be unavailable. For instance, you shouldn't include
both the standard paladin character class and the paladin prestige class
in the same game.
</p>
<h6>
UNIQUE SPELLS
</h6>
The <a style="color: #579eb6" href="bardSpells.html">bard</a>, <a style="color: #579eb6" href="paladinSpells.html">paladin</a>, and <a style="color: #579eb6" href="rangerSpells.html">ranger spell lists</a> contain a number of spells that
don't appear on other classes' spell lists. In general, any character who
enters one of these prestige classes should gain access to spells unique
to that class's spell list, at the same levels indicated for the standard
class. At the game master's discretion, spells unique to that class's
spell list found in other books may also be available, but on a case by
case basis. The game master may require such spells to be researched or
learned specifically by the character, rather than simply making them
freely available.
<h5><a id="prestige-bard"></a>
PRESTIGE BARD
</h5>
The prestige bard is a jack-of-all-trades, master of none. She relies on
wit, agility, and no small amount of magic to see her through her daring
adventures.
<p>
Virtually all characters who become prestige bards are already multiclass
characters, usually mixing <a style="color: #579eb6" href="rogue.html">rogue</a> and <a style="color: #579eb6" href="wizard.html">wizard</a> or <a style="color: #579eb6" href="rogue.html">rogue</a> and <a style="color: #579eb6" href="sorcerer.html">sorcerer</a>. Some
dabble in <a style="color: #579eb6" href="barbarian.html">barbarian</a> or <a style="color: #579eb6" href="fighter.html">fighter</a> as well, depending on their social
backgrounds.
</p>
<p>
NPC prestige bards may serve as heralds, tale-tellers, spies,
ne'er-do-wells, or all of the above. Some work in troupes, others form
colleges, but most share a strong streak of independence.
</p>
<p>
Compared to the <a style="color: #579eb6" href="bard.html">standard character class</a>, the prestige bard gains her
bardic music abilities later in her career, but has a greater range of
spells available.
</p>
<p><b>Hit Die:</b> d6.</p>
<h6>Requirements</h6>
To qualify to become a prestige bard, a character must fulfill all the
following criteria.

<b>Alignment:</b> Any nonlawful.

<b>Base Attack Bonus:</b> +3.

<b>Skills:</b> Knowledge (any one) 4 ranks, Perform (any one) 8 ranks.

<b>Special:</b> Ability to cast at least one 1st-level arcane spell from each of the
divination, enchantment, and illusion schools.
<h6>Class Skills</h6>
The prestige bard's class skills (and the key ability for each skill) are
<a style="color: #579eb6" href="skillsAll.html#appraise">Appraise</a> (Int), <a style="color: #579eb6" href="skillsAll.html#balance">Balance</a> (Dex), <a style="color: #579eb6" href="skillsAll.html#bluff">Bluff</a> (Cha), <a style="color: #579eb6" href="skillsAll.html#climb">Climb</a> (Str), <a style="color: #579eb6" href="skillsAll.html#concentration">Concentration</a>
(Con), <a style="color: #579eb6" href="skillsAll.html#craft">Craft</a> (Int), <a style="color: #579eb6" href="skillsAll.html#decipher-script">Decipher Script</a> (Int), <a style="color: #579eb6" href="skillsAll.html#diplomacy">Diplomacy</a> (Cha), <a style="color: #579eb6" href="skillsAll.html#disguise">Disguise</a>
(Cha), <a style="color: #579eb6" href="skillsAll.html#escape-artist">Escape Artist</a> (Dex), <a style="color: #579eb6" href="skillsAll.html#gather-information">Gather Information</a> (Cha), <a style="color: #579eb6" href="skillsAll.html#hide">Hide</a> (Dex), <a style="color: #579eb6" href="skillsAll.html#jump">Jump</a>
(Str), <a style="color: #579eb6" href="skillsAll.html#knowledge">Knowledge</a> (all skills, taken individually) (Int), <a style="color: #579eb6" href="skillsAll.html#listen">Listen</a> (Wis),
<a style="color: #579eb6" href="skillsAll.html#move-silently">Move Silently</a> (Dex), <a style="color: #579eb6" href="skillsAll.html#perform">Perform</a> (Cha), <a style="color: #579eb6" href="skillsAll.html#profession">Profession</a> (Wis), <a style="color: #579eb6" href="skillsAll.html#sense-motive">Sense Motive</a> (Wis),

<b>Skill Points at Each Level:</b> 6 + Int modifier.

<h6><a id="prestige-bard-class-features"></a>
Class Features
</h6>
All the following are class features of the prestige bard class.
<p><b>Weapon and Armor Proficiency:</b> As the standard bard. A prestige bard can cast any arcane spells in light
armor without the normal arcane spell failure chance.</p>
<p><b><a id="prestige-bard-spellcasting"></a>Spellcasting:</b> When a new prestige bard level is gained (except at 1st, 3rd, 7th, and
13th level), the character gains new spells per day (and spells known, if
applicable) as if she had also gained a level in whatever arcane
spellcasting class she belonged to before she added the prestige class.
</p>
<p>
A prestige bard casts arcane spells from the schools of divination,
enchantment, and illusion at +2 caster level. She casts arcane spells from
the schools of evocation and necromancy at -2 caster level.
</p>
<p><b><a id="bardic-knowledge"></a>Bardic Knowledge (Ex):</b>As the <a style="color: #579eb6" href="bard.html#bardic-knowledge">standard bard ability</a> of the same name.
</p>
<p><b><a id="bardic-music"></a>Bardic Music:</b> As the <a style="color: #579eb6" href="bard.html#bardic-music">standard bard ability</a> of the same name, except that the minimum
number of ranks in <a style="color: #579eb6" href="skillsAll.html#perform">Perform</a> required is increased by 5 (8 ranks for
<a style="color: #579eb6" href="bard.html#countersong">countersong</a>, <a style="color: #579eb6" href="bard.html#fascinate">fascinate</a>, or <a style="color: #579eb6" href="bard.html#inspire-courage">inspire courage</a>; 11 ranks for <a style="color: #579eb6" href="bard.html#inspire-competence">inspire
competence</a>, and so forth).</p>
<h5><a id="prestige-paladin"></a>
PRESTIGE PALADIN
</h5>
After training in the arts of combat and the mysteries of the divine, the
prestige paladin is anointed as a holy warrior dedicated to the protection
of law and goodness.
<p>
Most prestige paladins are <a style="color: #579eb6" href="cleric.html">clerics</a> or <a style="color: #579eb6" href="fighter.html">fighter</a>/<a style="color: #579eb6" href="cleric.html">clerics</a>, though rare
<a style="color: #579eb6" href="cleric.html">cleric</a>/<a style="color: #579eb6" href="monk.html">monks</a> might pursue this path as well.
</p>
<p>
NPC prestige paladins usually belong to a holy order dedicated to a lawful
good deity. Some are lone wanderers, dealing out justice while walking the
earth.
</p>
<p>
Compared to the <a style="color: #579eb6" href="paladin.html">standard paladin</a>, the prestige paladin gives up a small
amount of combat prowess in exchange for a larger spell list and a higher
top end of his spellcasting powers. Also, his class features aren't quite
as powerful as the standard paladin's as a result of his reduced paladin
class level.
</p>
<p><b>Hit Die:</b> d10.</p>
<h6>Requirements</h6>
To qualify to become a prestige paladin, a character must fulfill all the
following criteria.

<b>Alignment:</b> Lawful good.

<b>Base Attack Bonus:</b> +4.

<b>Skills:</b> <a style="color: #579eb6" href="skillsAll.html#knowledge">Knowledge</a> (religion) 2 ranks, <a style="color: #579eb6" href="skillsAll.html#knowledge">Knowledge</a> (nobility and royalty) 2 ranks,
<a style="color: #579eb6" href="skillsAll.html#ride">Ride</a> 4 ranks.

<b>Feats:</b> <a style="color: #579eb6" href="featsAll.html#mounted-combat">Mounted Combat</a>.

<b>Spellcasting:</b> Ability to cast <a style="color: #579eb6" href="spellsPtoR.html#protection-from-evil">protection from evil</a> as a divine spell.

<b>Special:</b> Ability to <a style="color: #579eb6" href="specialAttacks.html#turn">turn undead</a>.

<h6>Class Skills</h6>
The prestige paladin's class skills (and the key ability for each skill)
are <a style="color: #579eb6" href="skillsAll.html#concentration">Concentration</a> (Con), <a style="color: #579eb6" href="skillsAll.html#craft">Craft</a> (Int), <a style="color: #579eb6" href="skillsAll.html#diplomacy">Diplomacy</a> (Cha), <a style="color: #579eb6" href="skillsAll.html#handle-animal">Handle Animal</a>
(Cha), <a style="color: #579eb6" href="skillsAll.html#heal">Heal</a> (Wis), <a style="color: #579eb6" href="skillsAll.html#knowledge">Knowledge</a> (nobility and royalty) (Int), <a style="color: #579eb6" href="skillsAll.html#knowledge">Knowledge</a>
(religion) (Int), <a style="color: #579eb6" href="skillsAll.html#profession">Profession</a> (Wis), Ride (Dex), and <a style="color: #579eb6" href="skillsAll.html#sense-motive">Sense Motive</a> (Wis).

<b>Skill Points at Each Level:</b> 2 + Int modifier.

<h6><a id="prestige-paladin-class-features"></a>
Class Features
</h6>
All the following are class features of the prestige paladin class.
<p><b>Weapon and Armor Proficiency:</b>As the <a style="color: #579eb6" href="paladin.html">standard paladin</a>.</p>
<p><b><a id="prestige-paladin-spells-per-day"></a>Spells per Day:</b> A prestige paladin's training focuses on divine spellcasting. At every odd
numbered level reached, the prestige paladin gains new spells per day as
if he had also gained a level in whatever divine spellcasting class he
belonged to before he added the prestige class.
</p>
<p><b><a id="prestige-paladin-detect-evil"></a>Detect Evil (Sp):</b>As the <a style="color: #579eb6" href="paladin.html">standard paladin</a> ability of the same name.
</p>
<p><b><a id="prestige-paladin-smite-evil"></a>Smite Evil (Su):</b> As the <a style="color: #579eb6" href="paladin.html#paladin-smite-evil">standard paladin ability</a> of the same name.</p>
<p><b><a id="prestige-paladin-turn-undead"></a>Turn Undead (Su):</b> For the purpose of determining his ability to <a style="color: #579eb6" href="specialAttacks.html#turn">turn undead</a>, a prestige
paladin's class levels stack with his levels from all his other classes
from which he has gained the ability to turn undead. For instance, a
4th-level fighter/1st-level cleric/2nd-level prestige paladin would turn
undead as a 3rd-level cleric.
</p>
<p><b><a id="prestige-paladin-divine-grace"></a>Divine Grace (Su):</b> As the <a style="color: #579eb6" href="paladin.html#paladin-divine-grace">standard paladin ability</a> of the same name.</p>
<p><b><a id="prestige-paladin-lay-on-hands"></a>Lay on Hands (Su):</b> As the <a style="color: #579eb6" href="paladin.html#paladin-lay-on-hands">standard paladin ability</a> of the same name, except that the total
number of hit points of damage that a prestige paladin can heal per day is
equal to his Charisma modifier x (his paladin level + 3). For example, a
3rd-level prestige paladin with a Charisma of 16 (+3 modifier) can heal 18
points of damage per day.
</p>
<p><b><a id="prestige-paladin-special-mount"></a>Special Mount (Sp):</b> As the <a style="color: #579eb6" href="paladin.html#paladin-special-mount">standard paladin ability</a> of the same name, except treat the
prestige paladin's effective level as three higher when calculating the
mount's bonus Hit Dice, powers, and so forth.
</p>
<p><b><a id="prestige-paladin-aura-of-courage"></a>Aura of Courage (Su):</b> As the <a style="color: #579eb6" href="paladin.html#aura-of-courage">standard paladin ability</a> of the same name.</p>
<p><b><a id="prestige-paladin-divine-health"></a>Divine Health (Ex):</b> As the <a style="color: #579eb6" href="paladin.html#divine-health">standard paladin ability</a> of the same name.
</p>
<p><b><a id="prestige-paladin-remove-disease"></a>Remove Disease (Sp):</b> As the <a style="color: #579eb6" href="paladin.html#paladin-remove-disease">standard paladin ability</a> of the same name, except that a prestige
paladin gains the ability at 4th level and can use this ability one
additional time per week for every four levels gained thereafter (twice at
8th and three times at 12th).
</p>
<p><b><a id="prestige-paladin-code-of-conduct-and-associates"></a>Code of Conduct and Associates:</b> These restrictions are the same as those described for the <a style="color: #579eb6" href="paladin.html">standard
paladin class</a>.</p>
<h6><a id="ex-paladins"></a>Ex-Paladins:</h6> Same as for the <a style="color: #579eb6" href="paladin.html">standard paladin</a>, but with no restrictions on
multiclassing.

<h5><a id="prestige-ranger"></a>
PRESTIGE RANGER
</h5>
The prestige ranger navigates the dark forests, craggy mountains, or
desert wastes of her homeland with unparalleled skill. She becomes one
with nature while remaining a deadly hunter.
<p>
Most prestige rangers are former <a style="color: #579eb6" href="druid.html">druids</a>, though some multiclass in
<a style="color: #579eb6" href="barbarian.html">barbarian</a>, <a style="color: #579eb6" href="fighter.html">fighter</a>, or <a style="color: #579eb6" href="rogue.html">rogue</a>. Some replace druid levels with <a style="color: #579eb6" href="cleric.html">cleric</a>
levels, selecting the <a style="color: #579eb6" href="clericDomains.html#animal">Animal domain</a>. If your campaign uses the <a style="color: #579eb6" href="unearthedCoreClass.html#wilderness-rogue">wilderness
rogue</a>, that class also provides a good starting point for a would-be prestige ranger.
</p>
<p>
NPC prestige rangers are typically loners, trusting few but themselves
while on the hunt. Some form loose affiliations of like-minded
individuals, occasionally relying on each other for assistance in dire
times.
</p>
<p>
Compared to the <a style="color: #579eb6" href="ranger.html">standard ranger</a>, the prestige ranger gives up immediate
access to some of her iconic abilities (such as <a style="color: #579eb6" href="ranger.html#ranger-favored-enemy">favored enemy</a>) by having
to qualify for the class, but has a much wider selection of spells.
</p>
<p><b>Hit Die:</b> d8.</p>
<h6>
Requirements
</h6>
To qualify to become a prestige ranger, a character must fulfill all the
following criteria.

<b>Base Attack Bonus:</b> +4.

<b>Skills:</b> <a style="color: #579eb6" href="skillsAll.html#knowledge">Knowledge</a> (nature) 2 ranks, <a style="color: #579eb6" href="skillsAll.html#survival">Survival</a> 4 ranks.

<b>Feats:</b> <a style="color: #579eb6" href="featsAll.html#endurance">Endurance</a>, <a style="color: #579eb6" href="featsAll.html#track">Track</a>, and either <a style="color: #579eb6" href="featsAll.html#rapid-shot">Rapid Shot</a> or <a style="color: #579eb6" href="featsAll.html#two-weapon-fighting">Two-Weapon Fighting</a>.

<b>Spellcasting:</b> Must be able to cast <a style="color: #579eb6" href="spellsC.html#calm-animals">calm animals</a> as a divine spell.

<h6>
Class Skills
</h6>
The prestige ranger's class skills (and the key ability for each skill)
are <a style="color: #579eb6" href="skillsAll.html#climb">Climb</a> (Str), <a style="color: #579eb6" href="skillsAll.html#concentration">Concentration</a> (Con) <a style="color: #579eb6" href="skillsAll.html#craft">Craft</a> (Int), <a style="color: #579eb6" href="skillsAll.html#handle-animal">Handle Animal</a> (Cha),
<a style="color: #579eb6" href="skillsAll.html#heal">Heal</a> (Wis), <a style="color: #579eb6" href="skillsAll.html#hide">Hide</a> (Dex), <a style="color: #579eb6" href="skillsAll.html#jump">Jump</a> (Str), <a style="color: #579eb6" href="skillsAll.html#knowledge">Knowledge</a> (dungeoneering) (Int),
<a style="color: #579eb6" href="skillsAll.html#knowledge">Knowledge</a> (geography) (Int), <a style="color: #579eb6" href="skillsAll.html#knowledge">Knowledge</a> (nature) (Int), <a style="color: #579eb6" href="skillsAll.html#listen">Listen</a> (Wis), <a style="color: #579eb6" href="skillsAll.html#move-silently">Move
Silently</a> (Dex), <a style="color: #579eb6" href="skillsAll.html#profession">Profession</a> (Wis), <a style="color: #579eb6" href="skillsAll.html#ride">Ride</a> (Dex), <a style="color: #579eb6" href="skillsAll.html#search">Search</a> (Int), <a style="color: #579eb6" href="skillsAll.html#spot">Spot</a> (Wis),
<a style="color: #579eb6" href="skillsAll.html#survival">Survival</a> (Wis), <a style="color: #579eb6" href="skillsAll.html#swim">Swim</a> (Str), and <a style="color: #579eb6" href="skillsAll.html#use-rope">Use Rope</a> (Dex).

<b>Skill Points at Each Level:</b> 6 + Int modifier.

<h4><a id="prestige-ranger-class-featurtes"></a>
Class Features
</h4>
All the following are class features of the prestige ranger class.
<p><b>Weapon and Armor Proficiency:</b> As the <a style="color: #579eb6" href="ranger.html">standard ranger</a>.</p>
<p><b><a id="prestige-ranger-animal-companion"></a>Animal Companion (Ex):</b>At 1st level, a prestige ranger gains an animal companion. This is
identical to the <a href="druid.html#druid-animal-companion">standard druid ability</a> of the same name.</p>
<p>
If a prestige ranger already has an animal companion from another class,
the character's prestige ranger levels stack with those class levels for
purposes of determining the companion's abilities.
</p>
<p><b><a id="prestige-ranger-favored-enemy"></a>Favored Enemy (Ex):</b> As the <a style="color: #579eb6" href="ranger.html#ranger-favored-enemy">standard ranger ability</a> of the same name.</p>
<p><b><a id="prestige-ranger-wild-empathy"></a>Wild Empathy (Ex):</b> As the <a style="color: #579eb6" href="ranger.html#ranger-wild-empathy">standard ranger ability</a> of the same name.</p>
<p>
If a prestige ranger already has the wild empathy ability from another
class, the character's prestige ranger levels stack with those class
levels when making animal empathy checks.</p>
<p><b>Bonus Feat:</b> At 2nd level, a prestige ranger gains either Manyshot or Improved
Two-Weapon Fighting as a bonus feat. She must have the Rapid Shot feat to
select Manyshot as a bonus feat, and she must have Two-Weapon Fighting to
select Improved Two-Weapon Fighting as a bonus feat. However, the ranger
need not have any of the other normal prerequisites for the bonus feat
selected.
</p>
<p>
At 7th level, a prestige ranger gains either Improved Precise Shot or
Greater Two-Weapon Fighting as a bonus feat. She must have the Manyshot
and Rapid Shot feats to select Improved Precise Shot as a bonus feat, and
she must have Two-Weapon Fighting and Improved Two-Weapon Fighting to
select Greater Two-Weapon Fighting as a bonus feat. However, the ranger
need not have any of the other normal prerequisites for the bonus feat
selected.
</p>
<p><b><a id="prestige-ranger-woodland-stride"></a>Woodland Stride (Ex):</b> As the <a style="color: #579eb6" href="ranger.html#ranger-woodland-stride">standard ranger ability</a> of the same name.</p>
<p><b><a id="prestige-ranger-camouflage"></a>Camouflage (Ex):</b> As the <a style="color: #579eb6" href="ranger.html#ranger-camouflage">standard ranger ability</a> of the same name.</p>
<p><b><a id="prestige-ranger-hide-in-plain-sight"></a>Hide in Plain Sight (Ex):</b> As the <a style="color: #579eb6" href="ranger.html#ranger-hide-in-plain-sight">standard ranger ability</a> of the same name.</p>
