<h5><a
href="http://www.wizards.com/dnd/images/EPIC_Gallery/Gallery5a/44188_C5_StoneColossus.jpg"><img
class="art-wide" src="pict.jpg" title="" alt="pict"
style="border: 0px solid ; width: 73px; height: 75px;" /></a>STONE
COLOSSUS </h5>
<p>A stone colossus cannot speak or make any vocal noise. </p>
<h6>COMBAT </h6>
<p><span style="font-weight: bold;">Shatter (Su):</span> A stone
colossus can use <a style="color: rgb(87, 158, 182);"
href="spellsS.html#shatter">shatter</a>(DC 7) as a free action once
every 2 rounds
against an opponent&#8217;s weapon or other item as a 24th-level caster. The
DC is Charisma-based.</p>
<p><span style="font-weight: bold;">Magic Immunity (Ex):</span> A
stone colossus is immune to all spells, spell-like abilities, and
supernatural effects, except as follows. A <a
style="color: rgb(87, 158, 182);"
href="spellsTtoZ.html#transmute-rock-to-mud">transmute rock to mud</a>
spell
slows it (as the <a style="color: rgb(87, 158, 182);"
href="spellsS.html#slow">slow</a> spell) for 1 round, with no saving
throw, while <a style="color: rgb(87, 158, 182);"
href="spellsTtoZ.html#transmute-mud-to-rock">transmute mud to rock</a>
heals all its lost hit points. A <a style="color: rgb(87, 158, 182);"
href="spellsS.html#stone-to-flesh">stone to flesh</a>
spell has no effect on a stone colossus. </p>
<h6><span style="font-weight: bold;">Construction</span></h6>
<p>A stone colossus&#8217;s body is chiseled from a single block of
hard stone, such as granite, weighing at least 375,000 pounds. The
colossus costs 760,000 gp to create, which includes 100,000 gp for the
body. Assembling the body requires a successful&nbsp;<a
style="color: rgb(87, 158, 182);" href="skillsAll.html#craft">Craft</a>
(sculpting) or <a style="color: rgb(87, 158, 182);"
href="skillsAll.html#craft">Craft</a> (masonry) check (DC 37). The
creator must be 25th level and able
to <a style="color: rgb(87, 158, 182);"
href="epicFeats.html#epic-spellcasting">cast epic spells</a>.
Completing the ritual drains 30,600 XP from the
creator and requires <a style="color: rgb(87, 158, 182);"
href="spellsFtoG.html#geas-quest">geas/quest</a>, <a
style="color: rgb(87, 158, 182);" href="spellsHtoL.html#limited-wish">limited
wish</a>, <a style="color: rgb(87, 158, 182);"
href="spellsPtoR.html#polymorph-any-object">polymorph any object</a>,
and <a style="color: rgb(87, 158, 182);" href="spellsS.html#shatter">shatter</a>.


</p>
<h6><a
href="http://www.wizards.com/dnd/images/EPIC_Gallery/Gallery5a/44174_C5_FleshColossus.jpg"><img
class="art-wide" src="pict.jpg" title="" alt="pict"
style="border: 0px solid ; width: 73px; height: 75px;" /></a>FLESH
COLOSSUS </h6>
<p>A flesh colossus knows all languages spoken in life by the
ghost who acts as its controlling spirit. When the controlling spirit
is suppressed, it mutters in all the tongues spoken by its component
corpses. </p>
<h6>COMBAT </h6>
<p><span style="font-weight: bold;">Frightful Presence (Ex):</span>
All who come within a mile of the flesh colossus must make Will saves
(DC 55). Those who make their saves are <a
style="color: rgb(87, 158, 182);"
href="abilitiesAndConditions.html#shaken">shaken</a>. Those who fail
by 20
points or less are <a style="color: rgb(87, 158, 182);"
href="abilitiesAndConditions.html#frightened">frightened</a>. Those
who fail by 21 to 40 points are <a style="color: rgb(87, 158, 182);"
href="abilitiesAndConditions.html#panicked">panicked</a>. Those who
fail by 41 points or more are <a style="color: rgb(87, 158, 182);"
href="abilitiesAndConditions.html#cowering">cowering</a>. The DC is
Charisma-based.</p>
<p><span style="font-weight: bold;">Horrific Appearance (Su):</span>
Once per day, the controlling spirit within a flesh colossus may draw
upon the negative energy latent in the construct to transform its
countenance into a horrible leering visage: All within 300 feet who see
this must make a Fortitude save (DC 55) or take 2d4 points of temporary
Strength, Dexterity, and Constitution <a
style="color: rgb(87, 158, 182);"
href="abilitiesAndConditions.html#ability-damaged">damage</a>. The DC
is Charisma-based.</p>
<p><span style="font-weight: bold;">Stomp (Ex):</span> As a
full-round action, a flesh colossus may stomp upon any adjacent foe
(that is, any within the area it threatens). Its attack roll modifier
for this special attack is +65 (+75 base, -2 Dex, -8 size). If
successful, the stomp deals double damage (8d6 +24) and pins the victim
until the colossus chooses to release him or her. </p>
<p><span style="font-weight: bold;">Stench (Ex):</span> All
creatures with a sense of smell who come within 300 feet of the
construct must make a Fortitude save (DC 60) or be <a
style="color: rgb(87, 158, 182);"
href="abilitiesAndConditions.html#nauseated">nauseated</a> (unable to
attack, cast spells, concentrate on spells, or do anything else
requiring attention, except for a single move or move-equivalent action
per round). Those who succeed on their saves take a -4 circumstance
penalty on all attack rolls, saving throws, and skill checks for the
next 2d6+4 minutes. The DC is Constitution-based.</p>
<p><span style="font-weight: bold;">Magic Immunity (Ex):</span> A
flesh colossus is immune to all magical and supernatural effects except
those that use negative energy (see below). </p>
<p><span style="font-weight: bold;">Negative Energy Affinity (Su):</span>
Any negative energy spell targeted at the colossus repairs damage taken
by the colossus at the rate of 1d10 hit points per level of the spell. </p>
<h6>Construction </h6>
<p>Only a master necromancer of surpassing power can create a
flesh colossus. The bodies of not less than three hundred previously
animated Medium-size humanoid creatures must be stripped of their flesh
and the bones, which are then rendered in two great vats. The resulting
semiliquid bone-paste is then cast into a new articulated skeleton of
great size, to which the rendered flesh is applied like wet clay to
shape the figure. Finally, the necromancer must bind a <a
style="color: rgb(87, 158, 182);" href="monstersG.html#ghost">ghost</a>
of at
least 10 HD (the more powerful the better) into the colossus to act as
its controlling spirit. Without the controlling spirit, the colossus is
just an oversized <a style="color: rgb(87, 158, 182);"
href="monstersTtoZ.html#zombie">zombie</a>; with it, it functions as a
golem of great
size and repugnant appearance. If no ghost is available, the
necromancer may occupy the colossus himself by casting <a
style="color: rgb(87, 158, 182);" href="spellsMtoO.html#magic-jar">magic
jar</a>. The
colossus costs 760,000 gp to create, which includes 100,000 gp for the
body. Assembling the body takes no great skill, and many flesh colossi
reveal that fact in their uncouth forms. The creator must be 29th level
and able to <a style="color: rgb(87, 158, 182);"
href="epicFeats.html#epic-spellcasting">cast epic spells</a>.
Completing the ritual drains 30,600 XP
from the creator and requires <a style="color: rgb(87, 158, 182);"
href="spellsMtoO.html#magic-jar">magic jar</a>, <a
style="color: rgb(87, 158, 182);" href="spellsHtoL.html#limited-wish">limited
wish</a>, <a style="color: rgb(87, 158, 182);"
href="spellsPtoR.html#polymorph-any-object">polymorph any
object</a>, and <a style="color: rgb(87, 158, 182);"
href="spellsFtoG.html#minger-of-death">finger of death</a>.


</p>
<h5>IRON
COLOSSUS </h5>
<p><span style="font-weight: bold;">Breath Weapon (Su):</span>
Cone of poisonous gas, 80 feet, as a free action every 1d4 rounds,
initial damage 3d6 temporary Con, secondary damage 2d6 temporary Con,
Fort half (DC 58). The DC is Constitution-based.</p>
<p><span style="font-weight: bold;">Magic Immunity (Ex):</span>
An iron colossus is immune to all magical and supernatural effects,
except as follows. An electricity effect restores 1 hit point to the
iron colossus for each 3 points of damage it would otherwise deal. </p>
<p>Rustproof (Ex): An iron colossus is not affected by rust
attacks, unlike smaller <a style="color: rgb(87, 158, 182);"
href="monstersG.html#iron-golem">iron golems</a>. </p>
<h6>Construction </h6>
<p>An iron colossus&#8217;s body is sculpted from 150,000 pounds of
pure iron. The colossus costs 1,000,000 gp to create, which includes
100,500 gp for the body. Assembling the body requires a successful&nbsp;<a
style="color: rgb(87, 158, 182);" href="skillsAll.html#craft">Craft</a>
(armorsmithing) or <a style="color: rgb(87, 158, 182);"
href="skillsAll.html#craft">Craft</a> (weaponsmithing) check (DC 42).
The
creator must be 35th level and able to <a
style="color: rgb(87, 158, 182);"
href="epicFeats.html#epic-spellcasting">cast epic spells</a>.
Completing the
ritual drains 50,000 XP from the creator and requires <a
style="color: rgb(87, 158, 182);" href="spellsC.html#cloudkill">cloudkill</a>,
<a style="color: rgb(87, 158, 182);"
href="spellsFtoG.html#geas-quest">geas/quest</a>, <a
style="color: rgb(87, 158, 182);" href="spellsHtoL.html#limited-wish">limited
wish</a>, and <a style="color: rgb(87, 158, 182);"
href="spellsPtoR.html#polymorph-any-object">polymorph any object</a>.

<h3><a id="dragon-advanced"></a>DRAGON, ADVANCED</h3>
<table border="0" cellpadding="0" cellspacing="0" style="width: 100%">

<p>The standard advancement rules allow dragons theoretically
infinite progression even beyond the stage of great wyrm. The following
rules let dragons improve more than their Hit Dice as they progress to
epic heights of power.</p>
<p><span style="font-weight: bold;">Age Category:</span> A
standard dragon gains one &#8220;virtual age category&#8221; for every 3 Hit Dice
it gains beyond the great wyrm stage. Abilities that function once per
day per age category or otherwise use the dragon&#8217;s age category as part
of a calculation use this adjusted number. Epic dragons gain one age
category per 5 Hit Dice beyond great wyrm.</p>
<p><span style="font-weight: bold;">Size:</span> One important
element of dragon advancement is increasing size. The dragons that
don&#8217;t reach Colossal size by the great wyrm stage can never reach it
according to the standard advancement rules. When advancing a dragon,
consider its basic size group: lesser (white, black, brass, and copper
dragons), ordinary (green, blue, and bronze dragons), greater (silver,
red, and gold), or epic (force and prismatic). A dragon that is Tiny as
a wyrmling is in the lesser group, a dragon that is Small as a wyrmling
and never reaches Colossal size is in the ordinary group, and a dragon
that is Small to Large as a wyrmling and reaches Colossal by the great
wyrm stage is in the greater group. A lesser dragon becomes Colossal
when it gains two age categories (6 Hit Dice) beyond great wyrm. It
increases to Colossal+ when it gains an additional four age categories
(12 HD). An ordinary dragon becomes Colossal when it gains one age
category (3 Hit Dice) beyond great wyrm. It increases to Colossal+ when
it gains an additional four age categories (12 HD). A greater dragon
becomes Colossal+ when it gains four age categories (12 HD) more than
it needed to reach the Colossal size.


Epic dragons, already Colossal+ by the time they reach the great wyrm
stage, do not increase in size thereafter. Use the information
presented under the epic dragon entry, below, to determine the
statistics of a Colossal+ dragon.</p>

<p><span style="font-weight: bold;">Armor Class:</span> A
dragon&#8217;s natural armor bonus increases by +1 for every Hit Die it gains
beyond the great wyrm stage. (This rule applies for lesser dragon
advancement as well, since natural armor and Hit Dice always increase
at the same rate.)</p>
<p><span style="font-weight: bold;">Breath Weapon:</span> If a
dragon&#8217;s breath weapon deals damage, the damage typically increases by
2 dice for every virtual age category the dragon gains. The two
exceptions among standard dragons are the brass and white dragons,
whose breath weapon damage increases by only 1 die per age category.
The saving throw DC against a dragon&#8217;s breath weapon remains 10 + 1/2
the dragon&#8217;s Hit Dice + its Constitution modifier.</p>
<p><span style="font-weight: bold;">Spell Resistance:</span> For
standard advanced dragons, <a style="color: rgb(87, 158, 182);"
href="monsterTypes.html#SR">spell resistance</a> increases by 2 per
additional age category. For epic dragons, spell resistance increases
by 3 (for force dragons) or 6 (for prismatic dragons) per additional
age category.</p>
<p><span style="font-weight: bold;">Speed:</span> When a dragon
becomes Colossal, its fly speed increases by 50 feet and its
maneuverability becomes clumsy. When it reaches Colossal+, its fly
speed increases by another 50 feet, and its maneuverability remains
clumsy. A dragon&#8217;s land speed and other special movement types (swim,
burrow, and so on) do not change.</p>
<p><span style="font-weight: bold;">Ability Scores:</span> A
great wyrm&#8217;s Strength and Constitution scores both increase by +2 for
every virtual age category the dragon gains. Its Dexterity remains
unchanged. Its Intelligence, Wisdom, and Charisma increase by +2 for
every two age categories the dragon gains.</p>
<p><span style="font-weight: bold;">Special Abilities:</span>
Dragons do not gain additional spell-like abilities, but their <a
style="color: rgb(87, 158, 182);" href="monsterTypes.html#DR">damage
reduction</a> continues to increase as they gain Hit Dice. For most
dragons
(the exceptions being the force and prismatic dragons), the great
wyrm&#8217;s damage reduction increases to 15/epic after exceeding the great
wyrm age category and the dragon&#8217;s natural weapons count as epic
weapons for the purposes of bypassing damage reduction.


Force and prismatic dragons have the following DR progression: wyrmling
10/magic, young 15/magic, young adult 15/epic, mature adult 20/epic,
very old 25/epic.

</p>
<p><span style="font-weight: bold;">Caster Level:</span> A great
wyrm&#8217;s caster level increases by 2 for every virtual age category the
dragon gains. As with the epic dragons described later, advanced
dragons gain the&nbsp;<a style="color: rgb(87, 158, 182);"
href="epicFeats.html#improved-spell-capacity">Improved Spell Capacity</a>
feat as a bonus feat once for
every three caster levels above 20th.</p>

dragons, advanced dragons receive one feat for every 3 Hit Dice they

<h6>COMBAT </h6>

style="color: rgb(87, 158, 182);" href="monstersDrtoDw.html#dragon">standard
dragons</a>. Even wyrmling epic dragons are Huge, however, and are
capable
of making tail sweep and crush attacks. Although there is no size
category larger than Colossal, the oldest epic dragons have a greater
reach and deal more damage with their attacks than other Colossal
dragons, as shown on the Epic Dragon Face and Reach and Epic Dragon
Attacks tables below. In addition, the breath weapon of the oldest epic
dragons is a larger cone than most Colossal dragons possess. The size
modifier for these dragons remains -8.

dragon can choose <a style="color: rgb(87, 158, 182);"
href="epicFeats.html">epic feats</a> for which it otherwise meets the
prerequisites. Epic dragons also have access to dragon feats, as well
as the additional feats described in this book.


<span style="font-weight: bold;">Empower Spell-Like Ability:</span>
An epic dragon can apply the <a
  style="color: rgb(87, 158, 182);"
  href="featsAll.html#empower-spell"
>
  Empower Spell
</a>
feat to one of its spell-like abilities that it can use at least twice per day. If
the dragon has limited use of the spell-like ability, the empowered ability uses
up two uses of the ability. If the dragon can use the ability at will, there is no
cost for empowering the ability. The dragon does not need to specify a spell-like
ability when it chooses this feat; it can apply the feat to any spell-like ability
it possesses.

<span style="font-weight: bold;">Maximize Spell-Like Ability:</span>
The dragon can apply the <a
  style="color: rgb(87, 158, 182);"
  href="featsAll.html#maximize-spell"
>
  Maximize Spell
</a>
feat to one of its spell-like abilities that it can use at least three times per
day. If the dragon has limited use of the spell-like ability, the maximized ability
uses up three uses of the ability. If the dragon can use the ability at will, there
is no cost for maximizing the ability. The dragon does not need to specify a spell-like
ability when it chooses this feat; it can apply the feat to any spell-like ability
it possesses.

Epic dragons fly even faster than common dragons, and can cover greater
distances.

force that tears
through creatures and objects in its area to devastating effect. </p>

missiles</a>, <a style="color: rgb(87, 158, 182);"

other spell or effect with the Force descriptor. It can move freely
through barriers made of force, such as a <a
style="color: rgb(87, 158, 182);" href="spellsTtoZ.html#wall-of-force">wall
of force</a>. </p>
<p><span style="font-weight: bold;">Deflecting Force (Su):</span>
A force dragon is protected by a shield of force that provides a
deflection bonus to its AC. The deflection bonus is equal to the
dragon&#8217;s Charisma bonus. </p>
<p><span style="font-weight: bold;">Blur (Su):</span> When a
force dragon reaches juvenile age, the force surrounding its body
distorts the light around it so that it is hard to discern the dragon&#8217;s
exact location. This functions as a continuous <a
style="color: rgb(87, 158, 182);" href="spellsAtoB.html#blur">blur</a>
spell, giving the
dragon concealment (20% miss chance). </p>
<p><span style="font-weight: bold;">Displacement (Su):</span> An
old force dragon&#8217;s deflecting force bends light even more extremely,
giving the dragon the benefit of the <a
style="color: rgb(87, 158, 182);" href="spellsDtoE.html#displacement">displacement</a>
spell (50% miss
chance). This supersedes its blur ability. </p>
<p><span style="font-weight: bold;">Invisibility (Su):</span> A
great wyrm force dragon is a creature of pure force, and is completely <a
style="color: rgb(87, 158, 182);"
href="abilitiesAndConditions.html#invisible">invisible</a> (as though
protected by <a style="color: rgb(87, 158, 182);"
href="spellsHtoL.html#greater-invisibility">greater invisibility</a>).
A creature
that can see invisible creatures must still contend with the dragon&#8217;s
displacement ability.</p>

The DCs are Charisma-based.

<h5><a

style="border: 0px solid ; width: 73px; height: 75px;" /></a>PRIMAL AIR
ELEMENTAL </h5>
<p>Primal air elementals speak Auran. </p>
<h6>COMBAT </h6>
<p><span style="font-weight: bold;">Air Mastery (Ex):</span>
Airborne creatures take a -1 penalty on attack and damage rolls against
an air elemental. </p>
<p><span style="font-weight: bold;">Whirlwind (Su):</span> The
elemental can transform itself into a whirlwind once every 10 minutes
and remain in that form for up to 1 round for every 2 HD it has. In
this form, the elemental can move through the air or along a surface at
its fly speed.&nbsp; A primal air whirlwind is 10 feet wide at the
base, 40 feet wide at the top, and up to 80 feet tall. The elemental
controls its exact height, but it must be at least 10 feet. Gargantuan
or smaller creatures might take damage when caught in the whirlwind and
may be lifted into the air. An affected creature must succeed at a
Reflex save (DC 53) when it comes into contact with the whirlwind or
take 4d8 points of damage. It must also succeed at a second Reflex save
(DC 53) or be picked up bodily and held suspended in the powerful
winds, automatically taking 4d8 points of damage each round. A creature
that can fly is allowed a Reflex save (DC 53) each round to escape the
whirlwind. The creature still takes damage but can leave if the save is
successful. The elemental can eject any carried creatures whenever it
wishes, depositing them wherever the whirlwind happens to be. A
summoned elemental always ejects trapped creatures before returning to
its home plane. If the whirlwind&#8217;s base touches the ground, it creates
a swirling cloud of debris. This cloud is centered on the elemental and
has a diameter equal to half the whirlwind&#8217;s height. The cloud obscures
all vision, including darkvision, beyond 5 feet. Creatures 5 feet away
have one-half concealment, while those farther away have total
concealment. Those caught in the cloud must succeed at a <a
style="color: rgb(87, 158, 182);" href="skillsAll.html#concentration">Concentration</a>
check (DC 53) to cast a spell. The DC is Strength-based.


</p>

earth elemental gains a +1 attack and damage bonus if both it and its
foe touch the ground. If an opponent is airborne or water-borne, the
elemental takes a -4 penalty on attack and damage rolls. (These
modifiers are not included in the statistics block.) </p>
<p><span style="font-weight: bold;">Push (Ex):</span> An earth

<p>A primal fire elemental cannot enter water or any other

also catch fire unless they succeed at a Reflex save. The DC is
Constitution-based.

primal water elemental can be a serious threat to a ship that crosses
its path. It can easily overturn even large craft (ships of up to 5
feet in length per Hit Dice of the elemental) and stop massive vessels

succeed at a second Reflex save (DC 68) or be picked up bodily and held

damage each round. A creature that can swim is allowed a Reflex save

damage, but can leave if the save is successful. The elemental can
eject any carried creatures whenever it wishes, depositing them
wherever the vortex hap-pens to be. A summoned elemental always ejects
trapped creatures before returning to its home plane. If the vortex&#8217;s
base touches the bottom, it creates a swirling cloud of debris. This
cloud is centered on the elemental and has a diameter equal to half the
vortex&#8217;s height. The cloud obscures all vision, including dark-vision,
beyond 5 feet. Creatures 5 feet away have one-half concealment, while

<p>A genius loci is difficult to spot, since it looks like any
other section of landscape. A genius loci itself has no intelligence,
but rather &#8220;emulates&#8221; the sentience of any creature it currently
enslaves (see below), if any.&nbsp; Each genius loci has only a single
enslaved creature at one time, though other creatures may also inhabit
the surface of the genius loci. Any creature entering a genius loci who
makes a successful&nbsp;<a style="color: rgb(87, 158, 182);"
href="skillsAll.html#survival">Survival</a> check (DC 31) realizes
that something is
amiss, though it is difficult to tell exactly what. Genius loci do not
speak directly, though they may do so through their currently enslaved
thrall. </p>
<h6>COMBAT </h6>
<p>A genius loci can directly attack any creature standing on its
surface with animated parts of itself, delivering slam attacks. It also
directs its enslaved thrall against those it attacks. If its slave is
slain, it selects another of its foes as its next, newest slave. Once a
genius loci animates a portion of itself to attack the boundaries of
its form become clear, and the creature becomes open to attack
(<a style="color: rgb(87, 158, 182);" href="skillsAll.html#survival">Survival</a>
checks to determine the extent of the genius loci are no
longer necessary). The animated part of the genius loci has a reach of
15 feet. </p>
<p><span style="font-weight: bold;">Enslave (Su):</span> A genius
loci can use the epic spell <a style="color: rgb(87, 158, 182);"
href="epicSpells.html#enslave">enslave</a> once per round as a free
action
until it successfully enslaves a foe (Will DC 53 to resist). The DC is
Charisma-based. Once it has a slave, it cannot enslave another until
its current slave dies, is <a style="color: rgb(87, 158, 182);"
href="spellsHtoL.html#imprisonment">imprisoned</a>, or is otherwise
incapaticiated.
A slave becomes free if its genius loci master is slain. A genius loci
forces its slave to remain with it, though it may send its slave off to
temporarily visit nearby locations in order to draw in unsuspecting
victims. In fact, the &#8220;purposes&#8221; of the genius loci are to some degree
influenced by the current slave. The genius loci has no Intelligence
score of its own; thus, its emulation of its slave&#8217;s intelligence
sometimes transfers attitudes and goals. However, a slave can only go
so far in influencing its mobile master, and cannot knowingly attempt
to free itself from the genius loci that keeps it close. </p>
<p><span style="font-weight: bold;">Improved Grab (Ex):</span> If
the genius loci hits a Gargantuan or smaller creature with a slam
attack, it deals normal Damage and attempts to start a <a
style="color: rgb(87, 158, 182);" href="specialAttacks.html#grapple">grapple</a>
as a
free action without provoking an attack of opportunity. The genius loci
has the option to conduct the grapple normally, or simply use the part
of its body it used in the improved grab to hold the opponent. Each
successful grapple check it makes during successive rounds
automatically deals slam attack Damage, in addition to constriction
Damage. </p>
<p><span style="font-weight: bold;">Constrict (Ex):</span> A
genius loci crushes its opponent, dealing 4d10+30 points of bludgeoning
Damage, after making a successful <a style="color: rgb(87, 158, 182);"
href="specialAttacks.html#grapple">grapple</a> check. </p>
<p><span style="font-weight: bold;">Ooze Traits:</span> An ooze
is an amorphous or mutable creature. Oozes are immune to <a
style="color: rgb(87, 158, 182);"
href="abilitiesAndConditions.html#poison">poison</a>, sleep, <a
style="color: rgb(87, 158, 182);"
href="abilitiesAndConditions.html#paralyzed">paralysis</a>, <a
style="color: rgb(87, 158, 182);"
href="abilitiesAndConditions.html#stunned">stunning</a>, and
polymorphing. They have no clear front or back
and are therefore not subject to critical hits or flanking. Oozes are
blind but have the <a style="color: rgb(87, 158, 182);"
href="monsterTypes.html#blindsight">blindsight</a> special quality.
They have no
Intelligence scores and are therefore immune to all mind-affecting
effects (charms, compulsions, phantasms, patterns, and morale effects).

<p>A gibbering orb can speak all languages.</p>
<p>A gibbering orb&#8217;s natural weapons are treated as epic for the
purpose of overcoming <a style="color: rgb(87, 158, 182);"
href="monsterTypes.html#DR">damage reduction</a>.</p>
<h6>COMBAT </h6>
<p>The gibbering orb has the ability to bite its foes by
extending a pseudopod with one of its mouths protruding from the end.
The orb can extend four of these at any one foe, or a total of twelve
in any given round. At the same time, the legions of eyes have the
ability to cast a host of spells at a rapid rate. </p>
<p><span style="font-weight: bold;">Gibbering (Su):</span> The
cacophony of speech emanating from the scores of mouths that make up
the orb forces all within 60 feet of the creature to succeed at a Will
save each round (DC 40) or suffer the effects of an <a
style="color: rgb(87, 158, 182);" href="spellsHtoL.html#insanity">insanity</a>
spell. </p>
<p><span style="font-weight: bold;">Improved Grab (Ex):</span> To
use this ability, the gibbering orb must hit with a bite attack. If it
gets a hold, the orb automatically deals bite Damage and can attempt to
<a style="color: rgb(87, 158, 182);"
href="monsterTypes.html#swallow-whole">swallow whole</a>. </p>
<p><span style="font-weight: bold;">Swallow Whole (Ex):</span> A
gibbering orb can attempt to swallow a Large or smaller opponent by
making a successful <a style="color: rgb(87, 158, 182);"
href="specialAttacks.html#grapple">grapple</a> check. Once inside, the
opponent takes 4d8
points of constriction Damage plus 3d10 points of acid Damage per
round. A swallowed creature can cut its way out by using claws or a
light slashing weapon to deal 20 points of Damage in a single blow (AC
16, <a style="color: rgb(87, 158, 182);" href="monsterTypes.html#DR">DR</a>
10/epic still applies). Once the creature exits, the fluidlike
body structure of the orb closes the wound; other swallowed opponents
must cut their own way out. When a creature dies by being swallowed
whole (or when a creature killed by the gibbering orb in some other
fashion is eaten by it), the gibbering orb absorbs the creature&#8217;s known
spells, prepared spells, and spell-like abilities. The orb can use any
two of those abilities per round as a free action. Each originates from
an eye that is not producing an eye ray (see below) that round. Stolen
spells and spell-like abilities are lost after 24 hours. </p>
<p><span style="font-weight: bold;">Eye Rays (Su):</span> Two
dozen of the eyes can each produce a magical ray each round, with each
eye emulating a spell from among the list of spells below as if cast by
a 27th-level caster. The save DCs, where applicable, are 16 + spell
level. A gibbering orb has no directional limitations on where it can
point its eye rays, because the eyes orbiting around its body drift and
float wherever needed. However, a gibbering orb can never aim more than
five rays at any single target, due to limitations of aiming. All rays
have a range of 150 feet. Each of these effects functions as a ray,
regardless of the normal parameters of the spell it resembles. That is,
each is usable against a single target and requires a ranged touch
attack.
